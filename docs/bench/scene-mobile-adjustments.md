# Genesis Plaza → mobile readiness report

Data from A54 (Samsung Galaxy A54, Mali-G68 MP4 — near-minspec for our target tier). All numbers are HIGH graphic profile, GP preview, position 0,0, post-stack optimization branch.

## Observed bottlenecks (in order of impact)

### 1. Mesh sharing is broken end-to-end

GP has **~5,800 rendered surfaces** but the asset-server's mesh-dedup pass identified **only ~200 unique source meshes** (~213 shared-mesh hits over 1,065 GLBs in the import pipeline). Without explicit dedup, every MI ends up with a unique `ArrayMesh` → VBOs/IBOs duplicated 5–30× per source, materials & shader variants follow.

**Impact**: device GL memory ballooned to **1.88 GB** (Android `GL mtrack`) for content that's actually ~715 MB of unique geometry. The .scn output was **447 MB** vs **160 MB** after dedup (−64 %).

**Scene-side ask**:
- Re-use the same GLB across many parcels instead of duplicating per-instance variants (e.g. one `lamppost.glb`, instanced 50× via DCL transforms — not 50 near-identical lamppost GLBs).
- Where the same prop has minor variations (color tint, slight scale), prefer a **material override on a shared GLB** over a brand-new GLB.

### 2. Foliage / alpha-tested billboards eat a disproportionate share of GPU time

Bushes, leaves, grass tufts in GP are billboard quads with `Transparency::ALPHA_SCISSOR` materials. They:
- Cannot be auto-LOD'd safely (Godot's `generate_lods(60°, 25°)` collapses planar billboards into degenerate triangles → meshes disappear). Our stack now explicitly **skips transparent MIs** in the LOD bake.
- Disable early-z on the GPU; every alpha-test pixel runs the full fragment shader.
- Overdraw stacks when multiple foliage layers overlap, costing fillrate proportionally.

**Scene-side ask**:
- For bushes/grass: ship as **opaque cards with prebaked silhouette / cutout in albedo alpha**, not transparent billboards. Use a tight quad mesh that hugs the silhouette so alpha-tested pixels are < 50 % of the quad.
- Limit the number of foliage layers per parcel. Each layer at fullscreen is +2–4 ms of fragment cost on Mali-G68.
- Provide an `_lod1.glb` variant for foliage clusters (lower vertex count, larger alpha-test quads, fewer overlapping cards) — the client can swap automatically.

### 3. Visible primitive count is ~1.7 M per frame

Mali-G68 starts to choke above ~1 M primitives in view. We currently push the renderer hard. The LOD chain (LOD1=50 %, LOD2=25 %, LOD3=10 %) helps at distance but only kicks in when the screen-space error budget allows, which is rare at HIGH profile.

**Scene-side ask**:
- **Author meshes at mobile-appropriate triangle counts.** Aim for < 2 k tris per prop, < 20 k tris per building. The pipeline cannot decimate aggressively without breaking visuals (foliage proves this); the win has to come from source.
- **Provide explicit LOD GLBs** (`_lod0.glb`, `_lod1.glb`, `_lod2.glb`) for any building or large prop. The current LOD chain is auto-generated by `meshopt::simplify` + Godot's `generate_lods` — author LODs are always better.
- For repeated foliage / detail scatter, consider **GPU instancing via `MultiMeshInstance3D`** instead of per-tile MIs.

### 4. Draw call count is ~1,900 per frame

Each draw call has fixed CPU + Vulkan command-submission cost. On A54 at HIGH, we burn ~7–8 ms of `render_cpu_ms` per frame just submitting draws. Many of those are sub-100-triangle meshes that could be batched.

**Scene-side ask**:
- **Merge small static decorations** at authoring time (signs, small props, frames). One `street-furniture.glb` with all signage > 30 individual sign GLBs.
- Use **shared materials wherever possible** — the renderer batches by material; unique materials force unique state-changes.

### 5. Texture memory ~ 550 MB Godot-tracked / >1 GB on GPU

Most textures are 2048×2048 or 4096×4096. Mobile rarely needs > 1024 for prop textures and > 2048 for hero buildings. The texture-resize pipeline (#2078) caps at 4K but doesn't downsize most assets aggressively.

**Scene-side ask**:
- **Author textures at the resolution they'll actually be sampled at on mobile.** A 32 × 32 m building face seen from > 20 m away never needs > 1024 px.
- **Use compressed formats** for albedo (BC1/BC3 on desktop, ASTC 6×6 on mobile via the import pipeline) — already supported.
- **Avoid normal maps on small / distant props.** They double texture cost AND force per-fragment lighting (cheap-pbr's PER_VERTEX win disappears if the material has a normal map).
- **Avoid emissive textures** unless the prop is genuinely glowing — they're a fullscreen second pass on the bloom path.
- Combine albedo/roughness/metallic into a single packed texture (ORM) where possible.

### 6. Shadow casters are not curated

By default every MI casts shadows. The shadow pass roughly doubles vertex throughput cost for the directional light. After our shadow tweaks (#2036: 512 px, 30 m, 2-split cascade) the shadow map is reasonable, but most small props don't need to cast shadow.

**Scene-side ask**:
- **Mark small props / billboards / decorative geometry as `cast_shadow=OFF`** at authoring time. Trees, foliage, signs, indoor furniture — most don't need to cast.
- For large opaque buildings that DO cast shadow, that's fine — they're the cheapest per-vertex case.

### 7. Per-fragment PBR is expensive

Our `cheap-pbr` pass flips every BaseMaterial3D to `SHADING_MODE_PER_VERTEX`. That's a major win on Mali-G68 (fragment cost dominates), at the cost of softer specular highlights and crisper normal-map detail.

**Scene-side ask**:
- Don't expect crisp specular / normal-mapped detail on mobile. Author materials assuming **per-vertex lighting at runtime**: bake highlights into albedo, soften normal maps, prefer matte over glossy.
- If a prop NEEDS per-fragment shading for its identity (a metallic statue, glass), tag it explicitly so the client can opt that one out — the current `cheap-pbr` is binary (all or nothing).

### 8. Scene graph has ~20 k nodes

Every per-frame scene traversal pays a per-node cost. The visibility-grid V4 (opt-in tool in #1992) cuts this for our bench, but isn't default-ON.

**Scene-side ask**:
- **Flatten scene hierarchies** at authoring time. Nest only when nesting is load-bearing for animation / interactivity.
- Use **Decentraland's transform sharing** for repeated layouts (e.g. an avenue of identical buildings) rather than per-building wrapper entities.

## Quick-win checklist for scene authors (sorted by effort)

| Effort | Win | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 5 min | High | Mark non-essential MIs `cast_shadow=OFF` in the editor |
| 30 min | High | Resize textures > 1024 px on small props |
| 1 hr | Medium | Replace transparent foliage billboards with opaque alpha-cutout cards |
| 2 hr | High | Provide `_lod1.glb` for buildings > 5,000 tris |
| 1 day | Very high | Re-author repeated props as ONE GLB + MultiMesh instances |
| 1 day | Medium | Merge small static decorations into shared GLBs |

## What the client does for you (already in this stack)

- LOD chain auto-baked per opaque mesh via `meshopt::simplify` + Godot's `generate_lods`
- Shared meshes deduplicated across MIs (`apply_post_generate_godot_lods` memoization)
- BoxOccluder3D auto-spawned on big opaque meshes
- BaseMaterial3D forced to PER_VERTEX shading (cheap-pbr)
- Shadow map tuned to 512 px / 30 m / 2 cascades
- Asset-server baking moves all of the above to import-time → device pays zero cost

## What the client *cannot* fix for you

- Author-side mesh sharing (we dedup at the .scn level, but if the source GLBs are byte-different we can't tell they're "the same" thing)
- Source mesh triangle counts (we can decimate via LODs, but LOD0 — what you see at close range — is whatever you shipped)
- Texture authoring quality (we resize, but garbage-in / garbage-out for compression artifacts)
- Foliage rendering strategy (we can skip them from LOD, but the underlying alpha-test cost is fundamental)

## How to measure your own scene

```bash
# 1. Bring your scene live on a preview server, then point the bench at it:
adb shell am start -W -a android.intent.action.VIEW \
  -d "decentraland://?gp-benchmark=true&preview=http://YOUR_HOST:8000&position=X,Y&force-graphic-profile=3&cheap-pbr=true&fixed-skybox-time=true&bench-tag=your-scene"

# 2. Pull results:
adb pull /sdcard/Download/gp-benchmark/your-scene.json
adb pull /sdcard/Download/gp-benchmark/your-scene.png

# 3. Compare against baseline:
python3 scripts/bench/analyze_bench.py baseline.json your-scene.json
```

Key numbers to watch: `summary.fps.mean` (target ≥ 25 at HIGH), `summary.render_gpu_ms.mean` (target ≤ 33 ms for 30 fps), `summary.primitives.mean` (target ≤ 800 k visible), `summary.draw_calls.mean` (target ≤ 1,200).
